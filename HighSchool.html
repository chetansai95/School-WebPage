WELCOME TO OUR HIGH SCHOOL

Teaching Methodologies

1. Project-Based Learning:
  • Students work on real-world projects over an extended period, developing deep understanding and practical skills.
2. Blended Learning:
  • Combines traditional classroom teaching with online resources and activities, allowing for a more personalized learning experience.
3. Flipped Classroom:
  • Students study new content at home (e.g., through videos), then apply their knowledge in the classroom through discussions and activities.
4. Experiential Learning:
  • Learning through hands-on experiences, such as lab work, internships, and field trips, helping students connect theory to practice.
5. Socratic Method:
  • Teachers use questioning to stimulate critical thinking and dialogue, encouraging students to explore different perspectives.

Evaluation and Assessment

1. Formative Assessment:
  • Regular quizzes, class discussions, and homework that provide ongoing feedback to help students improve.
2. Summative Assessment:
  • End-of-term exams, major projects, or final papers that evaluate overall understanding of the course material.
3. Performance-Based Assessment:
  • Students demonstrate their knowledge through presentations, performances, or other creative outputs.
4. Standardized Testing:
  • State or national exams that measure student achievement against set standards, often used for college admissions.
5. Peer and Self-Assessment:
  • Encourages students to critique their own and others' work, developing critical thinking and self-awareness.
