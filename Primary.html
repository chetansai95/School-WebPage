WELCOME TO OUR PRIMARY SCHOOL

Teaching Methodologies

1. Play-Based Learning:
  • Children learn through play, which helps develop social skills, creativity, and cognitive abilities. Activities are designed to be fun and educational.
2. Inquiry-Based Learning:
  • Students are encouraged to ask questions and explore topics deeply. This method promotes critical thinking and problem-solving skills.
3. Differentiated Instruction:
  • Teaching is tailored to meet the diverse needs of students. Lessons are adapted to different learning styles, abilities, and interests.
4. Collaborative Learning:
  • Students work in groups to solve problems and complete tasks. This approach fosters teamwork, communication, and peer learning.
5. Technology-Enhanced Learning:
  • Digital tools and resources, such as educational apps and interactive whiteboards, are used to make learning more engaging and interactive.

Evaluation and Assessment

1. Formative Assessment:
  • Ongoing assessments during lessons, like quizzes and observations, help teachers understand student progress and adjust instruction accordingly.
2. Summative Assessment:
  • These are evaluations at the end of a unit or term, such as tests or projects, to measure studentsâ€™ understanding of the material.
3. Portfolio Assessment:
  • Students compile a portfolio of their work over time, showcasing their learning journey and achievements.
4. Peer and Self-Assessment:
  • Students assess their own or each other's work, which helps them develop self-reflection and critical thinking skills.
5. Standardized Testing:
  • These are formal tests that measure student performance against national or regional benchmarks.
