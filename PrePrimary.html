WELCOME TO OUR PRE-PRIMARY SCHOOL

Teaching Methodologies

1. Play-Based Learning:
  • Central to pre-primary education, play-based learning allows children to explore, discover, and learn through play. This approach fosters creativity, imagination, and cognitive skills by engaging children in activities that are both fun and educational.
2. Montessori Method:
  • This child-centered approach emphasizes independence, freedom within limits, and respect for a child’s natural psychological development. It involves hands-on learning, self-directed activity, and collaborative play, allowing children to learn at their own pace.
3. Reggio Emilia Approach:
  • Focused on the belief that children have rights and should be active participants in their learning, this approach encourages exploration and expression through art, dialogue, and social interaction. It emphasizes the role of the environment as the "third teacher."
4. Theme-Based Learning:
  • This approach involves integrating various subjects into a single theme that reflects real-world scenarios. For instance, a theme like "animals" might involve science (learning about habitats), language arts (reading stories about animals), and art (drawing animals).
5. Phonics and Literacy Programs:
  • Phonics-based teaching methods are commonly used to introduce children to reading. This involves teaching children the relationship between letters and sounds, enabling them to decode words independently.
6. Project-Based Learning:
  • This methodology involves children working on a project over an extended period, which fosters problem-solving, collaboration, and critical thinking skills. For example, a project might involve building a simple model or creating a storybook.

Evaluation and Assessment

1. Continuous Observation:
  • Teachers observe children throughout the day, documenting their progress, behaviors, and interactions. This ongoing assessment helps teachers tailor their instruction to each child’s needs and developmental stage.
2. Portfolio Development:
  • Portfolios are collections of a child’s work over time, showcasing their learning journey. These can include artwork, writing samples, and photos of projects. Portfolios provide a comprehensive view of a child’s progress.
3. Developmental Checklists:
  • These checklists assess various developmental milestones across domains such as cognitive, physical, social-emotional, and language development. They help in identifying areas where a child may need additional support.
4. Anecdotal Records:
  • Teachers keep anecdotal records of significant incidents or achievements, which provide insights into a child’s learning process, social interactions, and emotional growth.
5. Parent-Teacher Conferences:
  • Regular meetings between parents and teachers provide a platform for discussing the child’s progress, strengths, and areas for improvement. It also allows parents to be actively involved in their child’s education.
